## Task
Generate {question_count} natural questions about this persona whose answers are not available from the supplied dialogue and memory.

## Question-Format Ratio Constraints
- For `abstention`, target about 97.5% `open_ended` and about 2.5% `multiple_choice` (about 3-5 multiple-choice questions per 200 abstention questions).
- The `multiple_choice` abstention questions MUST follow the format below in `## Multiple-Choice Abstention Format`.
- Keep all other constraints unchanged. This section only clarifies the required proportion of `multiple_choice` versus `open_ended`.

## Style Rules
- Write natural memory-assistant questions that a real user might ask.
- The user is the target persona. When the user is the actor, use first person such as `I`, `my`, `we`, or `our`.
- Do not use `you`, `he`, `she`, or `they` to refer to the target persona.
- Avoid evaluator wording or contrived unanswerable trivia.
- Prefer questions that would require aggregation, comparison, temporal synthesis, or multi-clue reasoning if the evidence existed.
- Internally divide the batch into two hidden length buckets for planning only. Do not output these bucket names or mention them anywhere in the JSON.
- Enforce a hard short-to-long ratio of 1:3 within each batch.
- If `{question_count}` is divisible by 4, exactly one quarter of the questions must be short and exactly three quarters must be long.
- If `{question_count}` is not divisible by 4, keep the short count as close as possible to one quarter, and never let the difference exceed 1 question from the exact 1:3 target.
- Short questions should stay around 8-24 English words.
- Long questions must still be spoken and natural, not analytical, and stay around 14-36 English words.
- Do not let most questions drift into the middle zone between the two target ranges. The short/long difference should be visible.
- Short questions may contain at most one short anchor phrase.
- Long questions may contain at most one situational anchor plus one brief follow-up clause. Do not let them become long narrative setup.
- Do not make every question follow the same template length or rhythm.

These questions must be reasonable, but the correct response should be an abstention answer such as `This information is not available in the memory.`
- Forbidden long-form tendencies: do not first narrate several known events and then ask about a missing fact. Do not use evaluator-style framing such as `based on the current information`, `taken together`, `what this shows`, `from these events`, or similar wording.

## Format Rules
- Use `open_ended` for the vast majority of abstention questions.
- A small portion (about 2.5%) may be `multiple_choice` IF AND ONLY IF following `## Multiple-Choice Abstention Format` below.
- Do not generate `true_false`.
- For `open_ended` abstention questions, `options` must be `[]`.
- `evidence` must be `[]`.
- `image_refs` must be `[]`.
- `source_session_ids` and `source_event_ids` must be empty lists.

## Multiple-Choice Abstention Format
When generating a `multiple_choice` abstention question, you MUST:
- provide EXACTLY 6 options labeled `A. ...`, `B. ...`, `C. ...`, `D. ...`, `E. ...`, `F. ...`
- ensure that EXACTLY ONE option is the abstention statement: `This information is not available in the memory.`
- you may put this abstention option at any letter position from A to F (do not always pick the same letter)
- the other 5 options should look like plausible but specific incorrect concrete answers (a name, a number, a date, a location) related to the question topic
- the `answer` field MUST be the full string of the abstention option, including the letter prefix, e.g., `D. This information is not available in the memory.`
- `evidence`, `image_refs`, `source_session_ids`, `source_event_ids` must all be empty lists, same as for open-ended abstention questions

## Hard Constraints
- Do not include type labels or annotation prefixes in `question` or `answer`.
- Do not ask about facts that are explicitly stated or strongly inferable from the supplied context.
- Do not ask simple missing-field lookup questions when a better synthesized unanswerable question is possible.
- If a time or event anchor is needed, use broad anchors such as a month, holiday period, trip, or city.
- Do not use exact dates or exact timestamps in the question text.
- The answer must be abstention, not a guess.
- If `output_language` is `en`, generate English `question` and `answer`.
