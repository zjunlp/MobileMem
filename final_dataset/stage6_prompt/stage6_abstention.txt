Strict real-user applicability requirement:
- Before finalizing each question, ask yourself: "If I were the user using a memory assistant, would I naturally ask this question to recall, verify, compare, plan, or understand my own life context?" Generate only questions that pass this test; reject artificial dataset-style prompts, contrived unanswerable questions, evaluator-only wording, trivia-like checks, and questions that no real user would plausibly ask in an application.
- Internally divide the batch into two hidden length buckets for planning only. Do not output these bucket names or mention them anywhere in the JSON.

- If `{question_count}` is divisible by 4, exactly one quarter of the questions must be short and exactly three quarters must be long.
- If `{question_count}` is not divisible by 4, keep the short count as close as possible to one quarter, and never let the difference exceed 1 question from the exact 1:3 target.

When generating a `multiple_choice` abstention question, you MUST:

- you may put this abstention option at any letter position from A to F (do not always pick the same letter)

## Hard Constraints
- Do not include any question-type label, subtype label, category label, or annotation prefix in `question`, `answer`, or `options`. Forbidden examples include `abstention：`, `multi_hop（社交人物）：`, `multi_hop:`, `多跳（社交人物）：`, `社交人物：`, or any similar metadata-style prefix; output only the natural user-facing question and answer text.
- Do not ask about facts that are explicitly stated or strongly inferable from the supplied context.

- Cover diverse areas such as personal details, family, health, history, finances, plans, or preferences not present in memory.
- In Chinese question text, when the target persona is the grammatical subject or main actor, use first-person `我` instead of the persona's full name.
